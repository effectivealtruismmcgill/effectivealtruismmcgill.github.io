    Do good better

    By learning how to help others most effectively, you can transform your compassion into the best possible actions. Effective altruism equips the heart with the tools of the head.

    Knowing how to help

    Well-meaning efforts sometimes hurt the people they are intended to help. Often, they achieve nothing. But sometimes they save lives, relieve pain and suffering, and bring happiness to people in desperate situations.

    The world's poorest people have the world's greatest need

    Knowing how to help means knowing who to help. Helping the people who are the worst off means making the biggest difference. You can do that by donating to a charity that serves the world's poorest people.

    Not all charities are equally good at what they do

    If a company consistently fails to produce a good product, sooner or later customers will stop buying it. But if a charity consistently fails to achieve its goals, donors typically have no way of knowing.

    If you want to help, find the charities that are doing the best work

    Expert charity evaluators like GiveWell have the sole mission of finding the world's most effective charities. GiveWell's extensive research can help you decide where to donate.

    You can save a life by choosing the right charity

    If you're involved in a fundraiser, your choice of charity can save someone's life. A $3900 donation to the Against Malaria Foundation is enough to prevent one person from dying from malaria.
